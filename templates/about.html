About JJARQK Technologies

JJARQK started simply as an idea between 6 friends. Throughout America, fast food has become a plague on the public's health. JJARQK wanted to create a solution.

The Problem

Fast food, despite its potential health effects [1], is a common occurrence throughout America. According to the CDC, over one third of Americans eat fast food on a given day [2]. This is partially due to the high amount of fast food restaurants found throughout the country. One study [3] has shown that simply being exposed to fast food increases the likelihood of a person eating it. With inflation on the rise [4], fast food restaurants have increased their prices and are no longer the cheap option that they once were.

The Creation of JJARQK Hunt

In order to reduce fast food consumption, JJARQK created JJARQK Hunt, a database of user submitted restaurants within the $15 per person price range. Currently, JJARQK Hunt is completely non-profit, all costs coming out of pocket. Also, JJARQK belives the privacy of its users is of utmost importance, so, we promise to never include advertising in JJARQK Hunt.

Restaurant Vetting

JJARQK firmly believes that Hunt results should be as unbiased as possible, providing users with the information they need to make an informed choice about their eating habits. This is why all restaurants are vetted by human moderators before being added to the Hunt database. Moderators focus on two criteria that all restaurants must reach in order to be added to Hunt. The health review process is highly superficial, mainly ensuring the restaurant serves options other than "fast food", such as burgers, fries, or hot dogs.
